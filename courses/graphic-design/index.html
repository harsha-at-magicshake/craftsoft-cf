Course Curriculum

From design basics to building a professional brand identity and portfolio.

Beginner - Graphic Design Foundations

Goal: Build strong visual basics, design sense, and confidence with tools.

1 Design Fundamentals

  • What is Graphic Design & its role in branding and marketing
  • Design principles: balance, contrast, hierarchy, alignment, proximity
  • Visual perception & how users \"see\" design

2 Color & Typography Basics

  • Color theory, psychology, and combinations
  • Fonts vs typefaces, serif vs sans-serif
  • Typography rules: spacing, readability, pairing

3 Layout & Composition

  • Grids and layout systems
  • White space and visual flow
  • Designing for print vs digital

4 Tools Introduction

  • Adobe Photoshop basics
  • Adobe Illustrator basics
  • Working with shapes, layers, and artboards

Beginner Outcomes

  • Understand design language and terminology
  • Create clean posters, banners, and social creatives
  • Build confidence using design tools

Intermediate - Visual Communication & Branding

Goal: Move from \"good-looking\" designs to purposeful, brand-driven visuals.

1 Branding Essentials

  • What makes a brand visually strong
  • Logo types and usage
  • Brand colors, fonts, and consistency

2 Advanced Typography & Color

  • Type hierarchy for real-world designs
  • Emotional impact of colors
  • Advanced font pairing techniques

3 Design for Marketing

  • Social media creatives (Instagram, Ads, Thumbnails)
  • Posters, flyers, brochures
  • Digital vs print constraints

4 Tool Mastery

  • Photoshop: photo manipulation, retouching, mockups
  • Illustrator: logo design, icons, vector illustrations
  • Working with real brand briefs

Intermediate Outcomes

  • Design complete brand visuals
  • Create marketing-ready creatives
  • Think like a visual problem solver

Advanced - Professional Practice & Portfolio

Goal: Prepare for real clients, agencies, and freelance work.

1 Brand Identity Systems

  • Logo systems & variations
  • Brand guidelines (brand book creation)
  • Real-world branding case studies

2 Advanced Design Applications

  • Packaging design basics
  • Campaign creatives & visual storytelling
  • Design consistency across platforms

3 Portfolio Development

  • Selecting the right projects
  • Case study thinking (problem process solution)
  • Presentation for interviews & clients

4 Industry Readiness

  • Working with clients & feedback
  • Design workflows & handoff
  • Freelancing & agency expectations

Advanced Outcomes

  • Job-ready graphic design portfolio
  • Confidence to handle real clients
  • Clear understanding of industry standards
